# Revenue Recovery Assessment™
## Report Framework

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Revenue Recovery Assessment™ is designed to:

1. Identify hidden revenue leaks.
2. Benchmark the prospect against competitors.
3. Demonstrate financial impact.
4. Build trust and authority.
5. Generate consultation bookings.
6. Position our company as strategic advisors rather than software vendors.

---

# Psychology of the Report

The report is intentionally designed to trigger:

- Curiosity
- Loss aversion
- Fear of missing out (FOMO)
- Competitive instinct
- Hope and optimism

The goal is to encourage the prospect to continue reading and ultimately book a consultation.

---

# Report Structure

---

# Page 1
# Cover Page / Pattern Interrupt

Purpose:

Capture attention immediately.

Elements:

- Company Name
- Confidential Business Analysis
- Revenue Recovery Score™
- Competitor Average Score
- Estimated Revenue Left on the Table™
- Hook Statement

Examples:

⚠️ You May Be Losing $143,000 Per Year

⚠️ Your Competitors Are Outperforming You by 32%

⚠️ Your Business Is Missing Revenue Every Month

Call To Action:

Turn the page to discover why your competitors are winning and exactly how to close the gap.

---

# Page 2
# Executive Summary

Purpose:

Present the findings immediately.

Include:

- Revenue Recovery Score™
- Estimated Annual Revenue Leakage
- Biggest Operational Issues
- Competitor Comparison Snapshot
- Opportunity Score™

Goal:

Generate curiosity and emotional buy-in.

---

# Page 3
# Why Your Competitors Are Winning

Purpose:

Create urgency.

Include:

- Top competitors
- Ratings comparison
- Review count comparison
- Service comparison
- Customer sentiment comparison

Highlight positive competitor reviews that expose weaknesses in the prospect's business.

Examples:

> "They answered immediately."

> "Communication was excellent."

> "Received my quote within 15 minutes."

---

# Page 4
# Revenue Left on the Table™

Purpose:

Quantify the financial impact.

Include:

Current Estimated Revenue

Potential Revenue

Revenue Gap™

Examples:

Estimated Revenue Gap:

$170,000 per year

Question:

What would an additional $170,000 per year mean for your business?

---

# Page 5
# Revenue Leak Analysis

- Missed Calls
- Delayed Quotes
- Poor Communication
- Poor Reputation
- Inefficient Scheduling
- Lack of Follow-Up
- Poor Customer Experience

Estimate the financial impact of each issue.

# Competitor Selection Rules

Competitors must:

- Operate in the prospect's service area.
- Offer the same or very similar services.
- Have significant review activity.
- Be recognizable to the prospect.
- Be selected using objective criteria.

Do not use generic or unrelated competitors.

If sufficient competitor data cannot be found, the report should state:

"Additional competitor research is recommended before completing this section."
